Observation 001: Truth Collapse

Filed under: Observation

We used to agree, more or less, on what was real. What was recorded on tape, written in ink, captured in a photograph—these were once anchors. But the ground beneath them is eroding.

The rise of AI-generated content has accelerated a quiet, disorienting phenomenon: the collapse of truth. Not a destruction, but a melting. When everything can be synthesized convincingly, nothing needs to be verified. A news headline, a voice, a video—each becomes a possibility, not a certainty.

It’s not that the truth is gone. It’s that it's now one version among many, lost in a noise floor of plausible fictions. Trust, once granted to the image or the source, now floats unmoored. The observer becomes the filter—every claim, every post, every memory must be re-evaluated, again and again.

And in that constant scanning for signals of authenticity, we begin to doubt even our own judgment.

Is this what forgetting feels like?
